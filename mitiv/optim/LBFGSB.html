Class LBFGSB

    public class LBFGSB
extends java.lang.Object
implements ReverseCommunicationOptimizer
    Multivariate non-linear optimization with simple bound constraints by L-BFGS-B/VMLM-B method.

    There are some differences compared to LBFGS, the unconstrained version of the algorithm:

    1. The initial variables must be feasible. This is easily achieved by applying the projector on the initial variables.
    2. The gradients computed by the caller are projected. This means that they are not left unchanged.
    3. The line search procedure should only implement a sufficient decrease test (e.g. first Wolfe condition).

    Author:
    Éric Thiébaut.

      • getGradientThreshold

        public double getGradientThreshold()
        Query the gradient threshold for the convergence criterion. The convergence of the optimization method is achieved when the Euclidean norm of the gradient at a new iterate is less or equal the threshold: 
            max(0.0, gatol, grtol*gtest)
        where gtest is the norm of the initial gradient, gatol grtol are the absolute and relative tolerances for the convergence criterion.
        Returns:
        The gradient threshold.
        See Also:
        #setAbsoluteTolerance}, {@link #setRelativeTolerance}, {@link #getAbsoluteTolerance}, {@link #getRelativeTolerance}.
